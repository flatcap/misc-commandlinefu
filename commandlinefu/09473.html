Find all plain text files that do not contain STRING

find . -type f ! -exec grep -q 'STRING' {} \; -print

Hmm, that's a very clever use of find.

However GNU grep allows your example to be better expressed as:

grep -rL 'STRING' .

or

grep --recursive --files-without-match 'STRING' .

You need to escape the "!"
